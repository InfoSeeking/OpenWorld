# OpenWorld Roadmap

Phase 1 is currently underway at the University of Washington. This document describes planned deliverables for both phases; scope and timelines may shift as the project progresses.

---

## Phase 1 — Summer 2026 (3 months)

- Public benchmark suite: a curated set of physical scene clips with ground-truth annotations and an evaluation protocol that works without large-scale compute.
- Live demo comparing LLM, vision model, and world model reasoning on the same clips, hosted at [InfoSeeking.github.io/WorldModelDemo](https://InfoSeeking.github.io/WorldModelDemo/).
- Open GitHub repository with source code, benchmark data, schema documentation, and a primer for new contributors.

---

## Phase 2 — Fall 2026 through Spring 2027 (9 months)

- World adapter modules for three applied domains:
  - **Climate and environment** — scene understanding for weather events, land use change, and environmental monitoring.
  - **Health** — physical and procedural reasoning over clinical or care-setting video data.
  - **Agriculture** — crop state estimation, growth prediction, and anomaly detection from field imagery.
- Practitioner guide aimed at research groups and institutions without access to large-scale AI infrastructure, covering model selection, data preparation, and evaluation on constrained hardware.
- Peer-reviewed concept paper describing the OpenWorld framework, benchmark design, and findings from Phase 1 evaluations.
